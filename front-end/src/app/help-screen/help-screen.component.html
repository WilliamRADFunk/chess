Chess
Pawn Moves 01: A pawn can move one space (the opposite side of the bard from which it started), if not blocked by another piece.

Pawn Moves 02: For its first move, a pawn can move vertically two spaces if it isn't blocked by another piece.

Pawn Moves 03: A pawn can capture an opponent piece only by moving diagonoally one space in its permitted vertical direction.

Knight Moves: Any combination of two spaces vertically and one horizontally, or two horizontally and one vertically. Order doesn't matter.

The knight can "jump over" pieces, but can not "land" on it's own pieces.

Rook Moves 01: The rook moves any number of spaces along a straight line, vertically or horizontally.

Rook Moves 02: A rook is blocked by its own pieces. It can't jump pieces like a knight.

It can capture an enemy piece along those straight lines, but again can't jump pieces like the knight.

Bishop Moves 01: The bishop moves along a diagonal line in any of the four directions from its position.

Bishop Moves 02: A bishop is blocked by its own pieces. It can't jump pieces like a knight.

It can capture an enemy piece along those diagonal lines, but again can't jump pieces like the knight.
